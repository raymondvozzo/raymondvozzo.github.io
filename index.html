I am a Senior Lecturer at the University of Adelaide (soon to be Adelaide University!).

I’m interested in differential geometry and mathematical physics. In particular, my research has been focussed on bundle gerbes and infinite-dimensional geometry, and applications to quantum field theory and string theory. I also have an interest in mathematics education.

You can find links below to my papers in mathematics and education, as well as a list of graduate students I have supervised, together with their theses.

Some useful links:

  • My recent papers on MathSciNet (subscription required)
  • My recent preprints on the arXiv
  • Some of the expository tools I have created using Geogebra:
    • Powers of complex numbers (move a point around the complex plane and see how the square, inverse, etc. move around relative to it)
    • Eigenvectors and eigenvalues (vary the entries in a 2x2 matrix and see the effect of the corresponding linear transformation and see the eigenspaces)
    • Quadric surfaces (investigate the various quadric surfaces using cross sections)
    • Taylor Polynomials (enter a function, choose the order and view the polynomial and the error)
    • Indefinite integrals and antiderivatives (enter a function and see how the signed area corresponds to the indefinite integral)
    • Tangents to curves on surfaces (enter a surface and move along a curve on the surface and see the tangent to that curve)
    • Partial derivatives and tangent planes (enter a function and see the cross sections that give rise to the partial derivatives and see the tangent plane)
    • The blancmange function (shows the construction of the blancmange function; a continuous function that is nowhere differentiable)
    • Convergence of sequences (enter a sequence and choose a value of epsilon to see the terms that are within epsilon of the limit)
    • Newton's method (enter a function and choose an initial estimate for the zero; then choose how many iterations of Newton's method to show and see the tangents and the sequence of estimates for the zero)
